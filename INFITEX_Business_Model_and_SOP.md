# INFITEX GLOBAL ADVISORY
## Business Model & Standard Operating Procedures (SOP)
*An operating playbook for an India-based accounting-outsourcing + Digitech business serving Australian accounting practices.*

> **Important:** This is practical operating guidance, not legal, tax or financial advice. Contracts, data-protection obligations (Indian and Australian), tax registrations and insurance should be confirmed with a qualified lawyer and accountant in each country before you rely on them.

# PART A — THE BUSINESS MODEL

## A1. What the business is
INFITEX has two divisions that share one brand, one website and one sales engine:

1. **Accounting & Compliance Outsourcing** — white-label back-office capacity for Australian accounting/bookkeeping practices. You do the *preparation* work inside the practice's own systems (XPM, Xero, FYI Docs); the practice keeps the client relationship, the registered tax/BAS-agent status, and final sign-off.
2. **Digitech** — websites, domains, hosting, SEO, Google Business Profile, business email and maintenance for accounting practices and other small/medium businesses.

The two reinforce each other: an accounting client who trusts you with their books is a natural buyer of a website refresh, and vice-versa.

## A2. Who you serve (and who you don't)
- **Primary buyer:** owners/partners/managers of small-to-mid Australian accounting & bookkeeping practices who are short on staff during BAS/tax season.
- **Secondary buyer (Digitech):** any SME wanting a professional online presence.
- **Not your buyer:** direct end taxpayers who want lodgement/advice — you are not an Australian registered agent and must not position yourself as one.

## A3. Your boundary (keep this airtight)
- You provide **preparation and processing only**. The practice reviews, approves and lodges.
- You are **not** a registered Australian tax or BAS agent and give **no** tax advice to end clients.
- The practice's engagement letters with *their* clients should disclose that some work is performed by an overseas service provider (this is normal and expected under Australian privacy rules — see C7). Encourage your clients to keep that disclosure in place; it protects both of you.

## A4. Engagement (revenue) models
Offer a small menu so a practice can pick what suits them. Most competitors lead with one or two of these:

| Model | How it's priced | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **Dedicated resource / "seat" (FTE)** | Per person, per month | Firms with steady, ongoing volume who want the same people |
| **Managed / per-job (white-label)** | Fixed price per job/deliverable (e.g., per BAS, per return, per set of financials) | Firms that want the *result* without managing a person |
| **Block hours / overflow** | Pre-purchased hours, drawn down | Seasonal peaks (BAS quarters, EOFY/tax season) |
| **Digitech project** | Fixed project fee + optional monthly care/hosting retainer | One-off website builds with ongoing maintenance |

**Pricing principle (no prices on the website — "contact us for best pricing"):**
- Price from your **fully-loaded cost** (salary + on-costs + software + supervision + infrastructure + a security/quality buffer) **plus margin**, then sanity-check against the market.
- For context only, public market ranges for India→Australia accounting outsourcing are commonly quoted around **AUD ~$12–25/hour** or **~$500–1,500/month per dedicated resource**, varying by seniority and task complexity. Treat these as benchmarks, not targets — set your own numbers with your accountant.
- Whatever you quote, put **one all-inclusive number in writing**, state **no hidden setup fees** and **no long lock-in** if you can — transparency is a repeated selling point across the market.

## A5. How you win (differentiators to build into the brand)
Pulled from what Australian firms say they shortlist on:
- **Security-first** (this is now the deciding factor — see Part C and the website suggestions).
- **A real low-risk trial** (e.g., a free or fixed-fee pilot batch) so they can prove the work before committing.
- **A named single point of contact** who is a senior reviewer (the "pod"/account-manager model).
- **Maker–checker review** so nothing reaches the practice unreviewed.
- **Native fluency in the AU stack** (XPM, Xero, MYOB, FYI, Dext, BGL/Class) and the AU compliance calendar.
- **Retention/low attrition** — firms hate re-training; stability is a feature you can market.

# PART B — SERVICE DELIVERY SOP (STEP BY STEP)

A repeatable lifecycle. Each phase has an owner and a "done when" gate.

## Phase 0 — Lead & qualification *(Owner: Founder/Sales)*
1. Lead arrives (website form, WhatsApp, email, referral, LinkedIn).
2. Log it in a simple CRM (HubSpot/Zoho free tier).
3. Qualify: practice size, software (XPM/Xero?), work types, volumes, season pressure, decision-maker.
4. **Done when:** a discovery call is booked.

## Phase 1 — Discovery & scope *(Owner: Founder/Practice Manager)*
1. 30–45 min call: understand their jobs, standards, turnaround expectations, peak periods.
2. Map their work to your service list; identify the best engagement model (A4).
3. Send a short written **scope + proposal** (services, model, indicative turnaround, your security summary, next step).
4. **Done when:** they agree to a trial.

## Phase 2 — Contracts & paperwork *(Owner: Founder)*
Put these in place **before any access**:
- **MSA** (Master Services Agreement) — scope, model, fees, liability, term, no-poach.
- **NDA / Confidentiality Agreement**.
- **DPA** (Data Processing Agreement) — you act on the practice's instructions as a processor.
- **SLA** (Service Levels) — turnaround targets, accuracy target, review process, support hours.
- **Done when:** all signed and stored securely.

## Phase 3 — Secure access setup *(Owner: Team Lead + IT)*
1. The **practice** creates **named user logins** for your staff in *their* XPM/Xero/FYI (never share a single generic login).
2. Apply **least-privilege** roles (only what each person needs).
3. Enforce **MFA** on every account.
4. Decide the working environment (ideally a **locked-down virtual desktop / remote environment** so client data never lands on a personal device — see C2).
5. **Done when:** access verified, security checklist signed off.

## Phase 4 — Onboarding & SOP capture *(Owner: Team Lead)*
1. Walk-through with the practice of *their* way of working (templates, naming, checklists, who signs off).
2. Capture a **per-client SOP** (a one-page "how this firm likes it done").
3. Configure/confirm **XPM recurring jobs**, job categories and **job states**, and **FYI filing** rules.
4. Run a **trial batch** (a few jobs) and get feedback.
5. **Done when:** trial approved and BAU start date agreed.

## Phase 5 — Business-as-usual delivery (the daily engine) *(Owner: Preparers + Reviewer)*
For **every job**, follow the maker–checker flow and drive it through XPM job states:

1. **Planned → In Progress:** preparer picks up the job, opens the per-client SOP and checklist.
2. Do the work in the client's software; file every document in **FYI**, linked to the client + job.
3. Move to **Wait on Info** if something's missing → raise a single, clear query list (don't drip-feed).
4. **Wait on Review:** the **reviewer (senior)** runs the checklist, fixes/queries, ensures quality.
5. **Wait on Sign:** hand to the practice for approval/lodgement (they sign).
6. **Completed:** close the job; log time; note anything to improve.
7. Keep the practice updated with **daily/weekly status** (a shared tracker or their XPM dashboard).

## Phase 6 — Reporting & continuous improvement *(Owner: Practice Manager)*
- **Weekly:** status of open jobs, queries outstanding, anything at risk.
- **Monthly:** KPI pack — turnaround vs SLA, accuracy/rework %, volume, utilisation, what improved.
- **Quarterly:** relationship review; spot upsell (more seats, Digitech, new job types).

# PART C — DATA MANAGEMENT & SECURITY (THE MOST IMPORTANT PART)
Security is the #1 thing Australian firms screen on. Treat this as a product, not an afterthought. The goal: **client data stays in the client's environment, accessed by named people, with full traceability.**

## C1. Golden rules
- **Work *in* the client's systems** (XPM/Xero/FYI) — don't copy data out.
- **Least privilege** — each person sees only what their jobs need.
- **Named accounts only** — never shared logins; **MFA everywhere**.
- **No local data** — nothing saved to personal drives, desktops, email or USB.

## C2. Workspace & device controls
- Prefer a **secure virtual desktop (VDI)** or a hardened, company-controlled machine so files never persist locally.
- **Disable** USB/removable media and personal cloud drives on work machines.
- **Clean-desk / clean-screen**; auto-lock after inactivity; no phones-with-cameras at desks if you want to match higher-security clients.
- Company-managed devices with disk encryption, endpoint protection and patching (MDM if you scale).

## C3. Access & identity
- Central identity with MFA; unique strong passwords via a **password manager** (1Password/Bitwarden) — never plaintext, never shared in chat.
- **Joiners/movers/leavers** process: access granted on day 1 per role; **revoked same-day** on exit; quarterly access reviews.

## C4. Data in transit & at rest
- Everything over **encrypted channels** (HTTPS/TLS, VPN to the secure environment).
- No client data over personal email/WhatsApp. Use the practice's approved channels/portals.

## C5. Backups, retention & deletion
- You ideally hold **no** client data; where temporary files exist, define **retention** and **secure deletion** timelines.
- Document where any logs/working papers live and how long they're kept.

## C6. Confidentiality & culture
- Every staff member signs an **NDA** and an **Acceptable Use Policy**.
- Annual **security & privacy training**; phishing awareness; a no-blame **incident-reporting** habit.

## C7. Legal/compliance awareness (confirm with professionals)
- **Australian Privacy Act / APPs:** when an Australian practice sends personal information to an overseas processor, **APP 8 (cross-border disclosure)** applies. Practically, the practice should disclose overseas processing to *their* clients and take reasonable steps to ensure you handle data properly — your DPA and security posture make that easy for them. Build your DPA and security pack to *support* their obligation.
- **TPB (Tax Practitioners Board):** the registered agent (the practice) remains responsible for the work; your role is preparation/processing under their supervision. Keep that line clean in every document.
- **Indian side:** company registration, applicable Indian data-protection law, and contractual obligations to your clients.
- **Insurance:** consider **professional indemnity** and **cyber** cover.

## C8. Incident response (have this ready before you need it)
1. **Detect & contain** (isolate the account/device).
2. **Notify** the affected practice promptly per your SLA/DPA.
3. **Investigate & remediate** (root cause, fix).
4. **Record & learn** (post-incident review; update controls).

## C9. Certification roadmap (a sales accelerator)
Bigger competitors win on **ISO 27001** and **SOC 2 Type II**. You don't need them on day 1, but:
- **Now:** write and follow a real security policy (this section is your starting skeleton).
- **6–12 months:** close gaps, document controls, train staff.
- **12+ months:** pursue **ISO 27001** first (most recognised in AU). Market it the moment you have it.

# PART D — TEAM MANAGEMENT

## D1. Org structure (start lean, scale in pods)
```
Founder / Director
   └── Practice Manager (ops, clients, quality)
        ├── Team Lead / Senior Reviewer  ← single point of contact per client "pod"
        │     ├── Accountant / Tax preparer
        │     ├── Bookkeeper / BAS preparer
        │     └── SMSF / specialist (as needed)
        └── Digitech Lead
              ├── Web developer / designer
              └── SEO / digital specialist
```
A **"pod"** = a small team (reviewer + preparers) dedicated to a set of clients, with **one named senior** the practice talks to. This is a proven, marketable model.

## D2. Roles & responsibility (RACI, simplified)
| Activity | Preparer | Reviewer/Lead | Practice Mgr | Founder |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Prepare jobs | **R** | C | I | I |
| Quality review (maker-checker) | C | **R/A** | I | I |
| Client communication | I | **R** | A | C |
| Security compliance | R | R | **A** | A |
| Pricing & contracts | – | – | C | **R/A** |
*(R=Responsible, A=Accountable, C=Consulted, I=Informed)*

## D3. Hiring profile
- Commerce graduates, **CA/CPA (or part-qualified)**, with good written English.
- **Xero/MYOB certified** (or get them certified fast); exposure to AU compliance is a big plus.
- Screen for reliability and attention to detail over speed.

## D4. Training & enablement
- **Onboarding academy:** AU compliance basics (BAS/IAS, GST, PAYG, STP, SMSF, ASIC, Div 7A), the XPM/FYI/Xero stack, and your security policy.
- **Certifications:** Xero/MYOB/relevant SMSF tools (BGL/Class).
- **Ongoing:** monthly refreshers, AU tax-update sessions, per-client SOP training.
- Bigger players run formal academies and even visit client offices — training is a retention and quality lever.

## D5. Capacity, utilisation & timesheets
- Track time per job/client (also feeds pricing accuracy).
- Target a healthy **utilisation** (e.g., 75–85%) without burnout; plan extra capacity before BAS/EOFY peaks.
- Cross-train so no client depends on one person.

## D6. Performance KPIs (per person, per pod)
- **Turnaround time** vs SLA.
- **Accuracy / rework %** (jobs returned by reviewer or practice).
- **Utilisation %**.
- **SLA compliance %** and **query-response time**.
- **Client satisfaction** (quarterly check-in score).
Review monthly; coach, don't just measure.

## D7. Retention & culture (your competitive moat)
- Low attrition is something firms actively buy — protect it: fair pay, clear growth paths, recognition, stable pods, reasonable peak-season planning.
- Keep the same faces in front of each practice; continuity = trust.

## D8. Communication cadence
- **Daily:** internal stand-up (10 min) — what's in progress, blockers.
- **Within the day:** consolidated query lists to the practice (not constant pings).
- **Weekly:** client status update.
- **Monthly:** KPI pack + relationship check.

# PART E — TOOLING STACK (suggested, mostly low-cost)
- **Accounting/practice:** Xero Practice Manager (XPM), Xero, MYOB, FYI Docs, Dext/Hubdoc, BGL/Class (SMSF).
- **Internal workflow/PM:** the client's XPM + a light internal tracker (ClickUp/Asana/Trello).
- **Communication:** Microsoft Teams or Slack, Zoom/Google Meet, professional email.
- **Security:** password manager (1Password/Bitwarden), MFA app, VPN/VDI, endpoint protection, MDM (when you scale).
- **Sales/marketing:** your website + Web3Forms + WhatsApp/Email popups, a free CRM (HubSpot/Zoho), LinkedIn, a scheduling tool (Calendly) for "book a call."

# PART F — 30 / 60 / 90 DAY LAUNCH PLAN
**Days 0–30 — Foundation**
- Register the business; open accounts; get PI/cyber insurance quotes.
- Finalise website (contact details, domain, Search Console) and LinkedIn page.
- Draft MSA/NDA/DPA/SLA templates (lawyer-reviewed) and your security policy.
- Stand up the secure environment (VDI/devices, password manager, MFA).

**Days 31–60 — First clients**
- Outreach: LinkedIn + referrals + your site; offer a **low-risk pilot**.
- Land 1–2 pilot practices; run trial batches; capture per-client SOPs.
- Hire/confirm your first pod (reviewer + 1–2 preparers); train on AU stack + security.

**Days 61–90 — Prove & systemise**
- Convert pilots to ongoing engagements; collect (with permission) your first testimonial/case study.
- Lock in the maker-checker workflow and weekly/monthly reporting.
- Start the **ISO 27001** gap assessment; publish a "Data Security" page on the site.
- Begin Digitech cross-sell to your accounting clients.
