Privacy Policy

How INFITEX Global Advisory handles personal information collected through this website.

Last updated: 25 June 2026

This is a starting draft template, not legal advice. Please have it reviewed by a qualified lawyer in India and Australia, and tailor it to your final tools, entity details and practices before publishing.

1. Who we are

INFITEX Global Advisory (“INFITEX”, “we”, “us”) is an India-based provider of accounting & compliance outsourcing and digital (Digitech) services, working with accounting and bookkeeping practices in Australia. If you have any questions about this policy, contact us at hello@infitexglobal.com.

2. What this policy covers

This policy explains how we handle personal information that you provide through this website (for example, an enquiry form or a message you send us). It does not cover the client or end-customer information we process on behalf of an accounting practice as part of our outsourced services. In that role we act as a service provider (processor) on the practice’s documented instructions under a separate engagement agreement and data processing agreement, and that information is governed by those agreements and the practice’s own privacy policy.

3. Information we collect

Information you give us

  • Contact details you submit through the enquiry form, WhatsApp or email — typically the contact person’s name, business/practice name, email address, mobile number (including country code), your area of interest and any message you write.
  • Anything else you choose to send us when you get in touch or book a call.

Information collected automatically

  • Standard technical data such as IP address, browser and device type, and pages viewed, used to keep the site secure and working.
  • If web analytics are enabled, aggregated usage statistics, and cookies as described in section 11.

4. How we use your information

  • To respond to your enquiry, prepare a proposal or quote, and provide the services you ask about.
  • To send you the email digests or updates you choose to subscribe to (you can unsubscribe at any time).
  • To operate, secure and improve the website.
  • To meet our legal and regulatory obligations.

We do not sell your personal information.

5. Our basis for processing

We process your information with your consent (for example, when you submit the form or subscribe) and to pursue our legitimate business interests in responding to enquiries and running our business. You can withdraw consent or unsubscribe at any time by contacting us.

6. Sharing with service providers

We use trusted third parties to operate the website and our communications, and they process information only as needed to provide their service to us. These may include our form-handling provider, website hosting provider, messaging platforms (such as WhatsApp) when you choose to contact us there, an email provider, a scheduling tool, and a web-analytics provider. We may also disclose information where required by law or to protect our rights.

7. International handling of data

We operate from India, so enquiry information you submit through this site is received and processed in India. Where we process client or end-customer data as part of outsourced services for an Australian practice, cross-border handling is addressed in our engagement agreement and data processing agreement, and is designed to support the practice’s obligations under the Australian Privacy Principles (including APP 8 on cross-border disclosure). Applicable Indian data-protection law may also apply to our processing.

8. How we protect information

We use reasonable technical and organisational measures — including access controls, multi-factor authentication, encryption of data in transit, and confidentiality undertakings by our team — to protect personal information. No website or method of transmission is completely secure, so we cannot guarantee absolute security.

9. How long we keep it

We keep enquiry information only for as long as needed for the purpose it was collected, to maintain our business records, or as required by law — after which we delete or anonymise it.

10. Your choices and rights

You can ask us to access, correct or delete the personal information you have given us, or to stop sending you updates. Contact hello@infitexglobal.com and we will respond within a reasonable time. Depending on where you live, you may also have rights under local data-protection law.

11. Cookies

The website uses a small number of cookies and similar technologies — essential ones that make the site work (for example, remembering your light/dark theme preference) and, where enabled, optional analytics cookies that help us understand how the site is used. You can control or clear cookies through your browser settings; disabling some may affect how the site works.

12. Children

This website is intended for business users and is not directed to children under 18. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

13. Links to other sites

Our site may link to third-party websites and tools. We are not responsible for their content or privacy practices, and we encourage you to read their policies.

14. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. The “last updated” date above shows when it was last revised, and we will take reasonable steps to highlight significant changes.
